Hyderabad
\f1\b0  ({\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 /\uc0\u712 ha\u618 d\u601 r\u601 b\'e6d/}} {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key"}}{\fldrslt 
\f2\i \cf4 HY-d\uc0\u601 r-\u601 -bad}};\cf4 \super [5]\cf2 \nosupersub  {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO_15919"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 ISO}}: Haidar\uc0\u257 b\u257 d, Telugu: {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Telugu"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 [\uc0\u712 \u614 a\u618 \u815 da\u638 a\u720 ba\u720 d]}} {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Hyderabad.ogg"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 \super \uc0\u9432 }}, Urdu: {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Hindi_and_Urdu"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 [\uc0\u712 \u614 \u603 \u720 d\u601 \u638 a\u720 ba\u720 d]}}) is the capital and largest city of the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_state"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Indian state}} of {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telangana"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Telangana}}. It occupies 650\'a0km\super 2\nosupersub  (250\'a0sq\'a0mi) on the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deccan_Plateau"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Deccan Plateau}} along the banks of the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musi_River_(India)"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Musi River}}, in the northern part of Southern India. With an average altitude of 542\'a0m (1,778\'a0ft), much of Hyderabad is situated on hilly terrain around {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lakes_in_Hyderabad"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 artificial lakes}}, including the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hussain_Sagar"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Hussain Sagar}} lake, predating the city's founding, in the north of the city centre. According to the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011_census_of_India"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 2011 census of India}}, Hyderabad is the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_in_India_by_population"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 fourth-most populous city in India}} with a population of 6.9 million residents within the city limits, and has a population of 9.7 million residents in the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyderabad_Metropolitan_Region"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 metropolitan region}}, making it the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_metropolitan_areas_in_India"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 sixth-most populous metropolitan area}} in India. With an output of US$\'a095 billion,\cf4 \super [6][7]\cf2 \nosupersub  Hyderabad has the sixth-largest urban economy in India.\cb1 \
\cb3 The Qutb Shahi dynasty's {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Quli_Qutb_Shah"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah}} established Hyderabad in 1591 to extend the capital beyond the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golconda_Fort"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 fortified Golconda}}. In 1687, {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Golconda"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 the city was annexed}} by the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_Empire"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Mughals}}. In 1724, {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asaf_Jah_I"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Asaf Jah I}}, the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viceroy_of_the_Deccan"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Mughal viceroy}}, declared his sovereignty and founded the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asaf_Jahi_dynasty"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Asaf Jahi dynasty}}, also known as the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nizam_of_Hyderabad"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Nizams}}. Hyderabad served as the imperial capital of the Asaf Jahis from 1769 to 1948. As the capital of the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyderabad_State"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 princely state of Hyderabad}}, the city housed the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Residency,_Hyderabad"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 British Residency}} and {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secunderabad_Cantonment_Board"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 cantonment}} until {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Independence_Act_1947"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Indian independence}} in 1947. {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annexation_of_Hyderabad"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Hyderabad was annexed}} by the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominion_of_India"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Indian Union}} in 1948 and continued as a capital of {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyderabad_State_(1948%E2%80%931956)"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Hyderabad State}} from 1948 to 1956. After {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gentlemen%27s_Agreement_of_1956"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 the introduction}} of the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/States_Reorganisation_Act,_1956"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 States Reorganisation Act of 1956}}, Hyderabad was made the capital of the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh_(1956%E2%80%932014)"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 newly formed Andhra Pradesh}}. In 2014, {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh_Reorganisation_Act,_2014"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Andhra Pradesh was split}} to form the state of Telangana, and Hyderabad became the joint capital of the two states with a transitional arrangement scheduled to end in 2024.\cf4 \super [8]\cf2 \nosupersub  Since 1956, the city has housed the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashtrapati_Nilayam"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Rashtrapati Nilayam}}, the winter office of the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_India"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 president of India}}.\cb1 \
\cb3 Relics of the Qutb Shahi and Nizam eras remain visible today; the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charminar"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Charminar}} has come to symbolise the city. By the end of the early modern era, the Mughal Empire had declined in the Deccan, and the Nizam's patronage attracted men of letters from various parts of the world. {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Hyderabad"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 A distinctive culture}} arose from the amalgamation of local and migrated artisans, with {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deccan_painting"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 painting}}, {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bidriware"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 handicraft}}, {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jewels_of_the_Nizams_of_Hyderabad"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 jewellery}}, {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deccani_Masnavi"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 literature}}, {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deccani_language"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 dialect}} and {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clothing_of_Hyderabad,_India"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 clothing}} prominent even today. For {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyderabadi_cuisine"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 its cuisine}}, the city is listed as a {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City_of_Gastronomy"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 creative city of gastronomy}} by {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNESCO"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 UNESCO}}. The {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telugu_cinema"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Telugu film industry}} based in the city is the highest-grossing film industry in India as of 2021.\cb1 \
\cb3 Until the 19th century, Hyderabad was known for the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyderabad_pearls"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 pearl industry}} and was nicknamed the "City of Pearls", and was the only trading centre for {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golconda_diamonds"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Golconda diamonds}} in the world. Many of the city's historical and traditional {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bazaar"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 bazaars}} remain open. Hyderabad's central location between the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deccan_Plateau"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Deccan Plateau}} and the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Ghats"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Western Ghats}}, and industrialisation throughout the 20th century attracted major Indian research, manufacturing, educational and financial institutions. Since the 1990s, the city has emerged as an {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genome_Valley"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Indian hub of pharmaceuticals and biotechnology}} and information technology. The formation of the {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_economic_zone"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 special economic zones}} of {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hardware_Park,_Hyderabad"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 Hardware Park}} and {\field{\*\fldinst{HYPERLINK "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HITEC_City"}}{\fldrslt \cf4 HITEC City}}, dedicated to information technology, has encouraged leading multinationals to set up operations in Hyderabad.}
